# Dashboard Story — Executive Sales Review

*Written for leadership. The dashboard is designed to be read as one connected story, not separate charts.*

## 1. Executive summary
The business is healthy and growing — ₹217M in sales at a 15.3% margin (₹33M profit) across 2024–2025 — but its profit is **highly concentrated in Technology**, while **Furniture and deep discounting quietly erode value**. The biggest wins are not more sales; they are protecting Technology, fixing Furniture's economics, and disciplining discounts.

## 2. What is performing well
- **Technology**: 71% of sales at an 18.2% margin, delivering ~84% of total profit.
- **Top-line growth**: sales rise across the 24 months, peaking in Aug 2025.
- **Healthy AOV** (₹51,671) and a low overall return rate (4.5%).
- **South region** scales volume well and is a good template for others.

## 3. What is underperforming
- **Furniture**: high sales but only a 6.9% margin and the worst return rate (7.7%).
- **Deep-discount orders** (>30%): actually loss-making on average.
- **Home Office segment**: largest by sales but the highest return rate (5.7%).
- **Standard Class shipping**: the default mode is also the slowest (4.7 days).

## 4. What risks are visible
- **Profit concentration**: a single category (Technology) carries ~84% of profit — a shock there would hit the whole P&L.
- **Returns + thin margins in Furniture** compound each other.
- **Uncontrolled discounting** leaks profit at the deep end.

## 5. What opportunities are visible
- Lift **Furniture and Office Supplies margins** to diversify profit.
- **Cap discounts** to recover lost profit with little volume risk.
- Grow volume in **East/West** using the South playbook.
- Use **faster shipping** selectively to cut returns.

## 6. Recommended business actions
1. Defend and grow Technology (assortment, availability, marketing).
2. Restructure Furniture pricing/supplier terms and fix return drivers.
3. Cap routine discounts at ~20–25%; approval required above that.
4. Investigate Home Office returns; pilot faster shipping on returns-prone orders.

## 7. Limitations of the dashboard
- Shows **association, not causation** (e.g. discount vs profit is correlational).
- Two fields have minor missing values (customer_rating, campaign_channel).
- No cost-to-serve, inventory, or customer-lifetime data — returns cost is proxied by rate, not ₹.
- Covers 2024–2025 only; external factors (economy, competition) aren't included.

## 8. Suggested next analysis
- Quantify the **₹ cost of returns** by category and segment.
- Build a **discount-elasticity** model to set optimal caps.
- Analyse **customer lifetime value** by segment and channel.
- A/B test **shipping upgrades** against return rates.
