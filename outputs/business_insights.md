# Business Insights — Executive Sales Dashboard

Eight insights, each with observation → evidence → interpretation → recommended action. Together they cover sales trend, regional, category profitability, segment, discount, shipping, returns, and overall risk/opportunity.

## 1. Technology drives the vast majority of profit (Category profitability)
- **Observation:** Technology generates far more profit than Furniture or Office Supplies.
- **Evidence:** Technology = ₹153.9M sales (71% of total) at an 18.2% margin → ₹28.0M profit, **~84% of total company profit**. Office Supplies margin 14.9%; Furniture only 6.9%.
- **Interpretation:** The business is effectively a Technology business with two lower-margin lines attached.
- **Action:** Protect and grow Technology (assortment, availability, marketing); treat it as the strategic core.

## 2. Furniture is a margin and returns liability (Business risk)
- **Observation:** Furniture sells well but barely profits and is returned most.
- **Evidence:** ₹51.6M sales but only 6.9% margin (₹3.6M profit), and a **7.7% return rate vs 3.0% for Technology**.
- **Interpretation:** Furniture ties up sales volume at low profit and the highest return cost — a structural drag.
- **Action:** Review Furniture pricing, supplier terms, packaging/quality (returns), and consider rationalising the worst SKUs.

## 3. Discounts above 30% are loss-making (Discount impact)
- **Observation:** Profit per order falls steadily as discount rises and turns negative at deep discounts.
- **Evidence:** Avg profit/order: 0% → ₹13.2K, 11–20% → ₹6.3K, 21–30% → ₹3.2K, **>30% → −₹1.6K (loss)**. Correlation(discount, profit) = −0.27.
- **Interpretation:** Discounting buys volume at the expense of profit, and beyond ~30% it destroys value.
- **Action:** Cap routine discounts (e.g. ≤20–25%); require approval above that; redirect deep-discount budget to Technology.

## 4. Sales are growing, with a clear 2025 peak (Sales trend)
- **Observation:** Monthly sales trend upward across the 24 months.
- **Evidence:** Range from a ₹6.30M trough (Aug 2024) to a ₹10.86M peak (Aug 2025); 2025 months sit consistently above 2024.
- **Interpretation:** Underlying demand is expanding; mid-to-late months show seasonal strength.
- **Action:** Plan inventory and staffing for the recurring mid-year uplift; protect stock of Technology in peak months.

## 5. South leads, but regions are tightly bunched on margin (Regional performance)
- **Observation:** South is the largest region; margins are similar across all four.
- **Evidence:** South ₹64.7M sales (top), North ₹54.6M, others ~₹49M; margins all ~15–16%.
- **Interpretation:** Growth is volume-led by region, not margin-led — no region is structurally more profitable.
- **Action:** Use South as the scale model; pursue volume growth in East/West where headroom exists.

## 6. Home Office is the biggest segment but returns most (Customer segment)
- **Observation:** Home Office leads sales yet has the highest return rate.
- **Evidence:** Home Office ₹74.5M sales, **return rate 5.7% vs ~4.0% for Consumer/Corporate**.
- **Interpretation:** Home Office volume comes with more post-sale cost; net value is lower than the top-line suggests.
- **Action:** Investigate Home Office return drivers (product mix, expectations); tighten returns policy or product guidance for that segment.

## 7. Standard Class dominates shipping but is the slowest (Shipping/delivery)
- **Observation:** Most sales ship Standard Class, which is also the slowest.
- **Evidence:** Standard Class = ₹122.8M (57% of sales) at **4.7 avg delivery days**; Same Day averages 0.4 days with the lowest returns (2%).
- **Interpretation:** The default shipping mode is slow; faster modes correlate with fewer returns.
- **Action:** Test upgrading high-value or returns-prone orders to faster modes and measure the return-rate effect.

## 8. Overall: concentrated profit is the central risk and opportunity (Business risk/opportunity)
- **Observation:** Profit is highly concentrated in one category.
- **Evidence:** ~84% of profit from Technology; company margin 15.3%, return rate 4.5%, AOV ₹51,671.
- **Interpretation:** Strong overall, but a shock to Technology (supply, competition, margin pressure) would hit profit disproportionately.
- **Action:** Diversify profitable lines (lift Furniture/Office Supplies margins), defend Technology, and control discounting — the three levers with the clearest profit impact.
